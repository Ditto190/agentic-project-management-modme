# Discover & Envision Phase

## Purpose

Establish deep understanding of the client's business context, strategic objectives, and the problem space. Define the opportunity, assess feasibility, frame scope, and build the commercial and delivery foundation for the engagement.

## Guiding Principle

> Invest time upfront to define scope with precision. Ambiguity here compounds through all downstream phases. Every assumption must be surfaced, validated, or flagged as a risk.

## Key Questions

1. What problem are we solving and for whom?
2. What is in scope and explicitly out of scope?
3. What are the key assumptions underpinning our approach?
4. What constraints (technical, commercial, organisational) must we respect?
5. What does success look like for the client?
6. What are the critical dependencies and risks at this stage?

## Entry Criteria

- Client opportunity or RFP identified
- Initial stakeholder alignment confirmed
- Engagement sponsor identified

## Exit Criteria

- Scope baseline document signed off
- Assumptions and dependencies logged
- Initial RAID register established
- Delivery approach narrative drafted
- SoW or contract framework agreed

## Sub-Phases

1. **Manage Solution Planning** — Coordinate the overall planning effort
2. **Initialize** — Set up engagement infrastructure, team, and governance basics
3. **Confirm Scope** — Run scope workshops and baseline the scope
4. **Assess Capabilities & Constraints** — Technical, commercial, and organisational assessment

## Common Pitfalls

- **Scope ambiguity:** Never proceed without a written, signed scope definition. Verbal agreements are not baselines.
- **Optimistic timelines:** Apply contingency. Client stakeholders are almost always less available than planned.
- **Missing out-of-scope list:** The out-of-scope list is as important as the in-scope list for change control.
- **Assumption inflation:** Too many unvalidated assumptions = too many hidden risks.
